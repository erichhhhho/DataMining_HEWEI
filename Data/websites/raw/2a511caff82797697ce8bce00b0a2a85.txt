  <p>Villagers in southwest China have discovered a clump of massive mushrooms, a news website reported.</p>
<p>The fungi, some of which are 83.5cm high, were found by a farmer foraging for mushrooms near a village in Tengchong in Yunnan province, Yn.yunnan.cn reported.</p>
<p>“I’m 81 years old and have never seen such a big mushroom in my life,” the farmer was quoted as saying.</p>
<p>Villagers hailed the find as the “king of mushrooms”, the report said.<img title="An expert says the huge fungi are edible. Photo: Yunnan.net" width="660" height="385" data-resolution="2" data-original="https://cdn1.i-scmp.com/sites/default/files/images/methode/2017/10/23/e2e8a03c-b79d-11e7-affb-32c8d8b6484e_1320x770_145911.JPEG" class="caption" /></p>
<p>The largest has a diameter of 40cm, leading villagers to call them “elephant foot”.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.scmp.com/news/china/society/article/1988821/magic-mushrooms-chinese-villager-finds-massive-fungi-mountainside">Magic mushrooms: Chinese villager finds massive fungi on mountainside</a></p>
<p>Yang Zhuliang, a researcher at the Chinese Academy of Sciences’ Institute of Botany in Kunming, told the website the giant mushrooms were edible.</p>
<p>The farmer has put a fence around the mushrooms to protect them from visitors taking selfies and to stop them from eating them.</p>
<p>He also plans to preserve the fungi so more visitors can see them, the report said.</p>
