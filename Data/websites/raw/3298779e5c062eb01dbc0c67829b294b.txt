        <div class="body-copy">
            <p>You can always count on Tencent to entertain the masses, especially if it means stroking the ego of China&#x2019;s president.</p><div class="regwall" data-position="1"></div><aside class="inline-newsletter" data-state="ready"></aside><p>A day after the 19th Party Congress kicked off with a marathon <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/politics/articles/2017-10-18/xi-s-grand-vision-for-china-prioritizes-party-power-over-reform" title="Xi&#x2019;s Grandiose Vision for China Puts Party Power Over Reform (1)" target="_blank"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="StoryLink"><meta itemprop="suid" content="OY1MZJ6TTDS6">speech</a> by Xi Jinping, <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/700:HK" title="Company Overview"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="SecurityLink"><meta itemprop="url" content="bbg://securities/700%20HK%20Equity"><meta itemprop="security" content="700 HK Equity">Tencent Holdings Ltd.</a> rolled out a game to enlist its 1 billion users&#xA0;on WeChat in a clapping competition.&#xA0;After watching highlights of his three-and-a-half hour monologue, users have 19 seconds to repeatedly mash an &#x201C;applaud&#x201D; button superimposed on an iconic image of the Great Hall of the People.&#xA0;The game had garnered more than 800 million claps by Thursday morning -- a tribute to a seminal address that transfixed markets and was broadcast live on virtually every Chinese TV station Wednesday.</p><div class="regwall" data-position="2"></div><p>China&#x2019;s biggest internet companies -- by far the largest corporate sector to have thrived outside of government control --&#xA0;have scrambled in recent months to demonstrate fealty to the ruling apparatus. Their endeavors ranged from <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/BABA:US" title="Company Overview"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="SecurityLink"><meta itemprop="url" content="bbg://securities/BABA%20US%20Equity"><meta itemprop="security" content="BABA US Equity">Alibaba Group Holding Ltd.</a> touting cloud computing collaborations with local government to&#xA0;bike-rental giant Ofo setting up internal Party units.</p><div class="regwall" data-position="3"></div>
        <div id="box-6IPPkYK" class="page-ad" data-position="mobile-box">
            <script class="params" type="application/json">{"contentId":"OY1RWQ6TTDS101","position":"box","dimensions":{"mobile":[[300,250],[1,1]]},"type":"Mobile Body Box Ad","positionIncrement":1,"targeting":{"position":"box1","positionIncrement":1,"url":"/news/articles/2017-10-19/tencent-comes-up-with-a-novel-way-to-applaud-china-s-president"},"containerId":"box-6IPPkYK"}</script>
            <div class="mobile-box-ad-dummy"></div>

        <p>The race to pledge allegiance isn&#x2019;t surprising given the importance attached to the twice-a-decade congress, which is expected to&#xA0;set policy across every industry sector and help Xi consolidate his position as China&#x2019;s most-powerful leader in a generation. But Tencent, as the dominant powerhouse in social media and content, is in the spotlight.</p><div class="regwall" data-position="4"></div><figure data-type="image" data-id="319309088" data-thumbnail="https://assets.bwbx.io/images/users/iqjWHBFdfxIU/iPSfV9LQrShg/v0/460x260.jpg" align="center" data-align="center" style="max-width: 2000px;" class="figure-expandable"><div class="image"><div id="lazy-img-319309088" class="lazy-img"><style>#lazy-img-319309088:before{padding-top:66.7%;}</style><img class="aligncenter lazy-img__image" src="https://assets.bwbx.io/images/users/iqjWHBFdfxIU/iPSfV9LQrShg/v0/60x-1.jpg" alt="An attendee takes a photograph of Xi Jinping, China&apos;s president, as he pauses while giving a speech during the opening of the 19th National Congress of the Communist Party of China at the Great Hall of the People in Beijing, China, on Wednesday, Oct. 18, 2017. Photographer: Qilai Shen/Bloomberg" data-wp-imgselect="1" data-native-src="https://assets.bwbx.io/images/users/iqjWHBFdfxIU/iPSfV9LQrShg/v0/-1x-1.jpg"></div></div><figcaption><div class="news-figure-caption-text caption">

</figcaption></figure><p data-tout-type="story"><a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-10-08/the-meeting-that-could-seal-xi-s-grip-on-china-quicktake-q-a" title="Click for full story" target="_blank"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="StoryLink"><meta itemprop="suid" content="OXEMVQ6JIJUO">The Meeting That Could Seal Xi&#x2019;s Grip on China: QuickTake Q&amp;A</a></p><p>The company was dealt the &#x201C;<a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="/news/terminal/OWTWW36S9728" title="China Fines Tencent, Baidu, Weibo for Content Violations" class="terminal-news-story" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="StoryLink"><meta itemprop="suid" content="OWTWW36S9728">highest fines&#x201D;</a> by authorities in September for failing to supervise content on WeChat. Regulators declared creators of all online groups <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-09-12/china-s-latest-crackdown-on-message-groups-chills-wechat-users" title="China&#x2019;s Latest Crackdown on Message Groups Chills WeChat Users" target="_blank"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="StoryLink"><meta itemprop="suid" content="OW5J9S6TTDS0">responsible</a> for managing information -- even in private forums -- and began making arrests, sending chills through the country&#x2019;s web users. The government has long been <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-05-03/china-said-to-explore-taking-stakes-in-some-news-websites-apps" title="China Said to Explore Taking Stakes in Some News Websites (1)" target="_blank"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="StoryLink"><meta itemprop="suid" content="O6LBAN6TTDS8">said</a> to be exploring ways to strengthen their oversight by taking stakes in operators of video streaming sites and online portals, with Tencent being one of them.</p><p>Jane Yip, a spokeswoman for the company, didn&#x2019;t respond to an email and text message seeking comment.</p><p>As the social media giant&#x2019;s market value soars pass <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/700:HK" title="Company Overview"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="SecurityLink"><meta itemprop="url" content="bbg://securities/700%20HK%20Equity"><meta itemprop="security" content="700 HK Equity">$430 billion</a>, investors are betting on Tencent&#x2019;s ability to hew to national policies while still creating products that people like and rely on. In the meantime, some at least are having a blast at its latest game: one person was able to depress the &#x201C;applause&#x201D; button 60 times a second, a velocity just shy of a hummingbird&#x2019;s wings.</p><figure data-type="video" data-id="318849192" data-align="center" data-thumbnail="https://assets.bwbx.io/images/users/iqjWHBFdfxIU/ioXucsWBviKw/v1/220x126.jpg">
