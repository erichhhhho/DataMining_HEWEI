																														<p><b>Scientists in China succeeded in growing the yield of a strain of saltwater-tolerant rice nearly three times their expectation.</b></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In the spring months, over 200 types of rice were planted at the Saline-Alkali Tolerant Rice Research and Development Center in Qingdao, a coastal city in eastern China’s Shandong Province. Seawater from the Yellow Sea was pumped, diluted and channeled into the rice paddies.</span></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-115870" src="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/01-12.jpg" alt="" width="800" height="533" srcset="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/01-12.jpg 800w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/01-12-288x192.jpg 288w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/01-12-768x512.jpg 768w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/01-12-380x254.jpg 380w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/01-12-300x200.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" /></p><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Mobile_300x250_mid1"></div></center><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Desktop_300x250_mid1"></div></center></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Researchers initially expected an output of around 4.5 tons per hectare, but surprised themselves when four types registered 6.5 to 9.3 tons per hectare.</span></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-115871" src="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/09-5.jpg" alt="" width="800" height="533" srcset="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/09-5.jpg 800w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/09-5-288x192.jpg 288w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/09-5-768x512.jpg 768w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/09-5-380x254.jpg 380w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/09-5-300x200.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" /></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Tests on saline-alkali tolerant rice have been going on in China for some time, but the unexpected yield marks its readiness for commercialization.</span></p></p><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Mobile_300x250_mid2"></div></center><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Desktop_300x250_mid2"></div></center></p></p></p></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Research leader Yuan Longping, also known as China’s “Father of Hybrid Rice,” told reporters that cultivation of the strain can feed more than 200 million people, the </span><a href="http://www.scmp.com/news/china/society/article/2115250/chinese-scientists-put-rice-grown-seawater-nations-tables" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">South China Morning Post</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> noted.</span></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-115876" src="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/04-8.jpg" alt="" width="800" height="1201" srcset="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/04-8.jpg 800w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/04-8-128x192.jpg 128w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/04-8-768x1153.jpg 768w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/04-8-682x1024.jpg 682w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/04-8-512x768.jpg 512w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/04-8-719x1080.jpg 719w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/04-8-133x200.jpg 133w" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" /></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Qingdao-based startup Yuan Ce Biological Technology, which partnered with Yuan’s team, is now selling the new rice as “Yuan Mi” in honor of the scientist.</span></p></p></p><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Mobile_300x250_mid3"></div></center><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Desktop_300x250_mid3"></div></center></p></p></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">“Yuan Mi,” however, costs 50 yuan ($7.50) per kilogram — about eight times more than the cost of ordinary rice. It is currently sold in 1-kilogram (2.2 pounds), 2-kilogram (4.4 pounds), 5-kilogram (11 pounds) and 10-kilogram (22 pounds) packs.</span></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-115875" src="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/03-10.jpg" alt="" width="800" height="533" srcset="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/03-10.jpg 800w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/03-10-288x192.jpg 288w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/03-10-768x512.jpg 768w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/03-10-380x254.jpg 380w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/03-10-300x200.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" /></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Despite the hefty price tag, six tons of the strain have been sold since August, thanks to its impressive flavor and texture.</span></p></p></p></p><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Mobile_300x250_mid5"></div></center><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Desktop_300x250_mid5"></div></center></p></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In addition, consumers are reportedly keen on its potential health benefits.</span></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-115873" src="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/08-8.jpg" alt="" width="800" height="533" srcset="https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/08-8.jpg 800w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/08-8-288x192.jpg 288w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/08-8-768x512.jpg 768w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/08-8-380x254.jpg 380w,  https://nextshark-vxdsockgvw3ki.stackpathdns.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/08-8-300x200.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" /></p></p></p></p></p></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">According to </span><a href="http://news.xinhuanet.com/english/2017-09/29/c_136646444.htm" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Xinhua</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, the strain could be rich in calcium and other micronutrients, as such are abundant in saline water.</span></p></p></p></p></p><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Mobile_300x250_mid6"></div></center><center><div style="margin-bottom:10px;" id="NS_Desktop_300x250_mid6"></div></center></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The property of salt as a disinfectant could also repel pathogenic bacteria, making “sea rice” less exposed to pests. As a result, farmers may decrease their use of pesticides.</span></p></p></p></p></p></p>
