				<p>
<iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/jWdZm5fpcEQ" width="540"></iframe><p>
<p>
<span>China’s first space station, Tiangong-1,</span><span> </span><a href="http://www.iflscience.com/space/chinas-first-space-station-will-fall-to-earth-within-a-few-months/">is doomed to plummet</a><span> into Earth’s atmosphere within the coming months, IFL Science
reports.</span><span></span><p>
<p>
<span>Tiangong-1,
which means “The Heavenly Palace," was launched into orbit back in 2011.
This relatively small 10.3-meter-long (34-foot) space station was China’s first
space-laboratory module, making it a major milestone in their drive to become a
science superpower. </span><p>
<p>
<span>However, after fulfilling its historic mission, its
communication links fell silent in March 2016 and its mission was
terminated. Now, the space station is returning from the heavens
after idly orbiting Earth for the past few months.  </span><p>
<p>
<span><span>Jonathan McDowell, an astrophysicist at the Harvard-Smithsonian
Center for Astrophysics, told The Guardian on Friday: “Now that [its] perigee
is below 300 kilometers [186 miles] and it is in denser atmosphere, the rate of
decay is getting higher.”</span></span><span></span><p>
<blockquote><span>“I
expect it will come down a few months from now – late 2017 or early 2018.”
– Jonathan McDowell</span></blockquote><p>
<span>In August, the United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs
(UNOOSA) issued a statement </span><span>saying Tiangong-1 would re-enter at some point between
October 2017 and April 2018.</span><span></span><p>
<p>
<span>“Most
of the structural components of Tiangong-1 will be destroyed through burning
during the course of its re-entry,” added UNOOSA. “The probability of
endangering and causing damage to aviation and ground activities is very low.”</span><p>
<p>
<span>Wu
Ping, deputy director of China's manned space engineering office, also told a
press conference last year: "Based on our calculation and
analysis, most parts of the space lab will burn up during falling."</span><p>
<p>
<span>However,
it is possible that small pieces of debris no larger than 100 kilograms
(220 pounds) will reach Earth’s surface, according to McDowell. Far bigger
man-made objects have previously made uncontrolled re-entry into Earth's
atmosphere without too much drama. Most notably, Russia's space station Mir
– weighing a hefty 120,000 kilograms (265,000 pounds) –
plummeted to Earth in 2001.</span><p>
<p>



				<p>
<p>
<span>While
you should not expect a spectacular crash, it does mean you might be able to
see streaks of debris from Tiangong-1 burn up in Earth’s atmosphere. You can
track the location and progress of Tiangong-1 through the website
Heavens-Above, although the exact date of its return to Earth remains
unknown for now.</span><p>
<p>
<span><p>

        }

        $('.audioblock').html('');
    }
}

                    <p>The Chinese space station Tiangong-1 is falling towards Earth in an uncontrolled descent and will hit the planet sometime within the next few months or even days.

Chinese officials confirmed last year that they lost control of the country’s first orbiting space station when they failed to correct its altitude. 

According to the Guardian, officials initially predicted the space station would crash between October 2017 and April 2018, but Harvard University astrophysicist Jonathan McDowell said the crash could actually occur earlier in that time frame. 

“Now that [its] perigee is below 300km [186 miles] and it is in denser atmosphere, the rate of decay is getting higher,” McDowell told the Guardian. “I expect it will come down a few months from now – late 2017 or early 2018.”

Unfortunately, it’s nearly impossible to predict exactly when and where the crash will occur, McDowell said. In fact, a minor change in atmospheric conditions could move the landing site “from one continent to the next,” he said.

          function defineAspectRatio() {

            jQuery('.image-frame img').each(function(index) {

                jQuery(this).css('width', '100%');
                jQuery(this).css('height', 'auto');

                var parentWidth = jQuery(this).parent('.image-frame').width();
                var parentHeight = jQuery(this).parent('.image-frame').height();
                var imgWidth = jQuery(this).width();
                var imgHeight = jQuery(this).height();

                    if(imgWidth == imgHeight) {
                        jQuery(this).addClass('squa');

                        jQuery('#tracker').append('|' + imgWidth + '&' + parentWidth + '|');

                        if(parentHeight < parentWidth) {
                            jQuery(this).css('width', '100%');
                            jQuery(this).css('height', 'auto');
                        } else if(parentHeight > parentWidth) {
                            jQuery(this).css('height', '100%');
                            jQuery(this).css('width', 'auto');
                        }

                    } else if(imgWidth < imgHeight) {
                        jQuery(this).addClass('port');
                        jQuery(this).css('width', '100%');
                    } else if(imgWidth > imgHeight) {
                        jQuery(this).addClass('land');
                        jQuery(this).css('height', '100%');
                    }

                });

imageShimmy();

};

function imageShimmy() {

    jQuery('.image-frame img').each(function(index) {

        var parentWidth_sp = jQuery(this).parent('.image-frame').width();
        var parentHeight_sp = jQuery(this).parent('.image-frame').height();
        var imgWidth_sp = jQuery(this).width();
        var imgHeight_sp = jQuery(this).height();

        var imgWidthShimmy = (imgWidth_sp - parentWidth_sp)/2;
        var imgHeightShimmy = (imgHeight_sp - parentHeight_sp)/2;

                    if(jQuery(this).hasClass('land') && imgWidth_sp < parentWidth_sp) {
                        jQuery(this).css('width', '100%');
                        jQuery(this).css('height', 'auto');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin', '0');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin-top', '-' + imgHeightShimmy + 'px');
                    } else if(jQuery(this).hasClass('land') && imgWidth_sp > parentWidth_sp) {
                        jQuery(this).css('margin-left', '-' + imgWidthShimmy + 'px');
                    } else if(jQuery(this).hasClass('port') && imgHeight_sp < parentHeight_sp) {
                        jQuery(this).css('height', '100%');
                        jQuery(this).css('width', 'auto');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin', '0');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin-left', '-' + imgWidthShimmy + 'px');
                    } else if(jQuery(this).hasClass('port') && imgHeight_sp > parentHeight_sp) {
                        jQuery(this).css('margin-top', '-' + imgHeightShimmy + 'px');
                    } else if(jQuery(this).hasClass('squa') && imgHeight_sp < parentHeight_sp) {
                        jQuery(this).css('height', '100%');
                        jQuery(this).css('width', 'auto');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin', '0');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin-top', '-' + imgHeightShimmy + 'px');
                    } else if(jQuery(this).hasClass('squa') && imgHeight_sp > parentHeight_sp) {
                        jQuery(this).css('width', '100%');
                        jQuery(this).css('height', 'auto');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin', '0');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin-top', '-' + imgHeightShimmy + 'px');
                    } else if(jQuery(this).hasClass('squa') && imgWidth_sp < parentWidth_sp) {
                        jQuery(this).css('height', '100%');
                        jQuery(this).css('width', 'auto');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin', '0');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin-left', '-' + imgWidthShimmy + 'px');
                    } else if(jQuery(this).hasClass('squa') && imgWidth_sp > parentWidth_sp) {
                        jQuery(this).css('height', '100%');
                        jQuery(this).css('width', 'auto');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin', '0');
                        jQuery(this).css('margin-left', '-' + imgWidthShimmy + 'px');
                    };

                    jQuery(this).animate({
                        opacity: 1},
                        200, function() {
                        // stuff to do after animation is complete
                    });


                });
}
