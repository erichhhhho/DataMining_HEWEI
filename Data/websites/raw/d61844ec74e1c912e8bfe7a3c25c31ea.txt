                <h1 class="entry-title">Top Chinese officials &#8216;plotted to overthrow Xi Jinping&#8217;</h1>

                

                <div class="td-module-meta-info">

        <div class="td-post-featured-image"><a href="http://www.followcn.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/98398065_7cn.jpg" data-caption=""><img width="660" height="371" class="entry-thumb td-modal-image" src="http://www.followcn.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/98398065_7cn.jpg" srcset="http://www.followcn.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/98398065_7cn.jpg 660w, http://www.followcn.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/98398065_7cn-300x169.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 660px) 100vw, 660px" alt="" title="_98398065_7cn"/></a></div>
        <p class="story-body__introduction">A top Chinese official has claimed several high-ranking members of the Communist Party had plotted to seize power from President Xi Jinping.</p>
<p>These party members have since been arrested or jailed in a wide-reaching corruption crackdown launched by Mr Xi.</p>
<p>Some observers have said the crackdown was used to conduct a political purge of Mr Xi&#8217;s opponents.</p>
<p>The latest claim of a power struggle appears to contradict the Communist Party&#8217;s unified image.</p>
<ul class="story-body__unordered-list">
<li class="story-body__list-item"><a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/resources/idt-sh/Thoughts_Chairman_Xi">The Thoughts of Chairman Xi</a></li>
<li class="story-body__list-item"><a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.com/news/blogs-china-blog-41663623">A speech that spells power</a></li>
<li class="story-body__list-item"><a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-china-41250273">How China&#8217;s Communist Party picks leaders</a></li>
</ul>
<p>On Thursday, the chief of China&#8217;s securities commissions, Liu Shiyu, listed names of six &#8220;high-ranking and powerful officials, who were hugely greedy and hugely corrupt, and who plotted to usurp the party&#8217;s leadership and seize state power&#8221;.</p>
<p>Mr Liu&#8217;s list included top names whose downfalls have attracted intense scrutiny, such as former security chief <a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-china-26349305">Zhou Yongkang</a>, prominent politician <a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-china-19709555">Bo Xilai</a>, and <a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-china-40706213">Sun Zhengcai</a>, a member of the decision-making Politburo body who was only recently expelled.</p>
<figure class="media-landscape has-caption full-width"><span class="image-and-copyright-container"><img class="responsive-image__img js-image-replace" src="https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/news/624/cpsprodpb/12939/production/_98398067_b4696692-0704-4b00-857e-f7623b56e0ca.jpg" alt="Picture of Zhou Yongkang" width="976" height="549" data-highest-encountered-width="624" /></span><figcaption class="media-caption"><span class="media-caption__text">Mr Zhou was the chief of security in China until his arrest in 2014</span></figcaption></figure>
<figure class="media-landscape has-caption full-width"><span class="image-and-copyright-container"><img class="responsive-image__img js-image-replace" src="https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/news/624/cpsprodpb/17759/production/_98398069_a5d77453-02c6-4f04-ac35-1221ac14210a.jpg" alt="Picture of Bo Xilai" width="976" height="650" data-highest-encountered-width="624" /></span><figcaption class="media-caption"><span class="media-caption__text">Mr Bo&#8217;s case was linked to the murder of a British businessman</span></figcaption></figure>
<p>The other names were former presidential aide<a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-china-36703368"> Ling Jihua</a>, the late army general <a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-china-31901941">Xu Caihou</a>, and former top military officer <a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-china-36883608">Guo Boxiong.</a></p>
<p>&#8220;These cases were definitely shocking,&#8221; said Mr Liu, who was speaking at a panel for the ongoing Communist Party congress in Beijing.</p>
<p>It was unclear whether he was referring to one co-ordinated plot or separate attempts at a coup.</p>
<p>Mr Liu said that Mr Xi had &#8220;addressed these problems, and eliminated a huge and hidden danger to the party and the country&#8221;.</p>
<ul class="story-body__unordered-list">
<li class="story-body__list-item"><a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/resources/idt-sh/Murder_lucky_hotel">Murder in the Lucky Holiday Hotel</a></li>
<li class="story-body__list-item"><a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-china-41322178">The rising stars of China&#8217;s Communist Party</a></li>
</ul>
<p>More than one million officials have been jailed or arrested in the corruption crackdown which began shortly after Mr Xi took power in 2012.</p>
<p>The depth of the crackdown has led many to speculate that some arrests were politically motivated and that part of Mr Xi&#8217;s overall strategy was to consolidate power.</p>
<p>Mr Xi had <a class="story-body__link" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-china-34332954">previously denied</a> claims there was a &#8220;power struggle&#8221; within the party.</p>
<p>But the comments appear to corroborate what has long been suspected &#8211; that there are intense power struggles going on behind the party&#8217;s image of unity, says the BBC&#8217;s Michael Bristow.</p>
<p>The claim comes as Mr Xi created his own political ideology, in the latest move to entrench his position in the Communist Party.</p>
<p>The party is widely expected to rewrite its constitution to enshrine this theory before congress ends next week.</p>
<p>BBC</p>
